Allan A. Martell

I'm in the job market

I'm a postdoctoral researcher at the School of Library and Information Science at Louisiana State University. You can read my bio here.

My research lies at the intersection of interactions design, memory studies, and museum studies. In my work, I explore:

    1) how design decisions shape the way that people remember the recent violent past, and
     2) how to re-orient the design process to support critical, nuanced interpretations of that past.
     3) how to re-orient the design process to engender empathy towards the victims of past violence.

My dissertation: “Memory labor as memory-making: museum exhibition design as a mediation of memory,” examines the role of participatory museum exhibition design in reframing the memories of children of former war refugees.

I argue that exhibition design can serve to intervene in post-conflict contexts. Specifically, my findings indicate that participation in the design process leads grandchildren of former war refugees to remember differently. This occurs because design enables these children a sustained and interactive experience of deeply reflecting about their own memories while also engaging with similar recollections from like-minded peers and forces them to create tangible and public representations of their own memories.
